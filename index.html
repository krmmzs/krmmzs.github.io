Barnum effect

The Barnum effect, also called the Forer effect or, less commonly, the Barnum–Forer effect, is a common psychological phenomenon whereby individuals give high accuracy ratings to descriptions of their personality that supposedly are tailored specifically to them, yet which are in fact vague and general enough to apply to a wide range of people.[1] This effect can provide a partial explanation for the widespread acceptance of some paranormal beliefs and practices, such as astrology, fortune telling, aura reading, and some types of personality tests.[1]

巴纳姆效应，又称福勒效应，或不太常见的巴纳姆-福勒效应，是一种常见的心理现象，在这种心理现象下，个人会对所谓专门针对他们的人格描述给予很高的准确度评价，但事实上这些描述是模糊和笼统的，足以适用于各种各样的人。[1] 这种效应可以部分解释为什么一些超自然信仰和实践被广泛接受，如占星术、算命、灵气解读和某些类型的人格测试。

These characterizations are often used by practitioners as a con-technique to convince victims that they are endowed with a paranormal gift. Because the assessment statements are so vague, people ascribe their own interpretation, thus the statement becomes "personal" to them. Also, individuals are more likely to accept negative assessments of themselves if they perceive the person presenting the assessment as a high-status professional.

从业者常常利用这些特征作为一种骗术，让受害者相信他们拥有超自然的天赋。由于评估陈述非常含糊，人们会做出自己的解释，因此这些陈述就成了他们的 "个人 "陈述。此外，如果人们认为提出评估的人是地位较高的专业人士，他们就更有可能接受对自己的负面评估。

Researcher Bertram Forer originally named it the "fallacy of personal validation".[2] The term "Barnum effect" was coined in 1956 by psychologist Paul Meehl in his essay "Wanted – A Good Cookbook", because he relates the vague personality descriptions used in certain "pseudo-successful" psychological tests to those given by showman P. T. Barnum.[3][4]

心理学家保罗-米尔（Paul Meehl）于1956年在他的论文 "Wanted - A Good Cookbook "中创造了 "巴纳姆效应"（Barnum effect）一词，因为他将某些 "伪成功 "心理测试中使用的模糊人格描述与表演者巴纳姆（P. T. Barnum）的人格描述联系起来。

RTFM 之 man手册的使用

Info

The man pages, short for reference manual pages, are your keys to Linux. Everything you want to know is there – take it all in an run with it. The collection of documents will never win a Pulitzer prize, but the set is quite accurate and complete. The man pages are the primary source and that authority is well-known.

While they are the “go to” source, they aren’t the most pleasant to read. Once, in a long past philosophy class, I was told that reading Aristotle was the most boring read around. I disagreed: when it comes to dry reading, Aristotle comes in at a distant second to man pages.

At first glance, the pages may look incomplete but, believe it or not, the man pages aren’t designed to hide information from you – it’s just that there is so much information that the pages have to be structured and information is given in the most brief form possible. The explanations are rather spartan and they will take some getting used to, but once you get the hang of using them, you’ll see how useful they actually are.

How to use

There is an introduction to the man pages and it’s important that you read this one. It spells out, in great detail, how the man pages are laid out and organized.

  • "带有'*'标记的命令可以在前面跟一个数, 这时命令的行为在括号里给出"
  • NAME - 命令名
  • SYNOPSIS - 使用方法大纲
  • CONFIGURATION - 配置
  • DESCRIPTION - 功能说明
  • OPTIONS - 可选参数说明
  • EXIT STATUS - 退出状态, 这是一个返回给父进程的值
  • RETURN VALUE - 返回值
  • ERRORS - 可能出现的错误类型
  • ENVIRONMENT - 环境变量
  • FILES - 相关配置文件
  • VERSIONS - 版本
  • CONFORMING TO - 符合的规范
  • NOTES - 使用注意事项
  • BUGS - 已经发现的bug
  • EXAMPLE - 一些例子
  • AUTHORS - 作者
  • SEE ALSO - 功能或操作对象相近的其它命令

In Ubuntu, icons are stored in several directories, including:

  • /usr/share/icons: This directory contains system-wide icons that are installed by default as part of various packages.
  • /usr/local/share/icons: This directory contains icons that have been installed manually or by third-party software.
  • ~/.icons: This directory, located in the home directory of each user, contains custom icons that have been installed or created by individual users.

In addition to these directories, some applications may include their own icons in their installation directories. For example, icons for a specific application might be located in /usr/share/<application-name>/icons.

Note that the directories listed above may contain subdirectories, each of which may contain icons for a specific theme. For example, the /usr/share/icons directory may contain subdirectories such as Adwaita, Humanity, elementary, etc. each containing icons for a specific theme.

思维悖论：绘梨想看优太创作完成的最终版电影，可电影却要一直拍到绘梨死去，所以产生一个问题：死去的绘梨怎么看到这部完结的电影？ 最终树用了巧妙的方式解决了这个问题（伏笔）：吸血鬼。

曾和女友谈论过谁先死的话题，一方死去后另一方要以怎样的方式继续活下去，本质上也就是讨论如何面对别人的死亡。也许现在可以采用优太的方式：用电影记录，最后用一抹奇幻色彩告别。

藤本树

谈论作品肯定离不开作者本身。

藤本树想表达什么？

第1部电影优太自己唯一自主拍摄的只有"爆炸"，而那爆炸却不受大众喜欢。 第2部电影整部都不是自己喜欢的，是绘梨喜欢的，但大家却好评如潮。 最后的电影就是长大后以爆炸为结局的这部漫画，他最后的最后也要加入代表自己的那一抹奇幻色彩。

换作是藤本树，第1部电影可能是炎拳。 第2部电影可能是初期的电锯人，大众开始发现藤本树了而且喜欢他的作品，但早期的电锯人定位就是易懂而面向大众的，他可能到了中期不太满意 (所以后面的电锯人非常有藤本树风格)。

结局的可能是在表达藤本树对于有趣是如何定义的想法。一部生离死别的催泪的电影，这样可能是好电影（再莫名其妙我也会流泪），这样也符合主流价值观。但是藤本老师这样的结局可能 是想要表达：对不起，这就是我心中的有趣，这就是我心中的那一抹奇幻色彩（我爱b级小烂片）。
